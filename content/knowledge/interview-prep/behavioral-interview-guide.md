## The STAR Framework

| Element | What to include | Time |
|---------|----------------|------|
| **Situation** | Context — where, when, what was happening | 15% |
| **Task** | Your specific responsibility or challenge | 10% |
| **Action** | What YOU did (not the team) — be specific | 50% |
| **Result** | Quantified outcome + what you learned | 25% |

## Key Rules

1. **"I" not "we"** — they're evaluating you, not your team
2. **Quantify results** — "improved by 15%" beats "improved a lot"
3. **Recent examples** — last 2–3 years, ideally current role
4. **Prepare 8–10 stories** that can flex across multiple questions
5. **Practice out loud** — 2 minutes per story, max

## Top BQ Questions by Competency

### Impact & Results
- Tell me about a project that had the most impact on a product decision
- Describe a time your research changed the direction of a team
- What's the most important insight you've delivered?

### Collaboration & Influence
- Tell me about a time you had to convince a skeptical stakeholder
- Describe a disagreement with a PM/engineer about research findings
- How do you handle it when your recommendations aren't followed?

### Ambiguity & Problem-Solving
- Describe a time you had to figure out a research approach for an unclear question
- Tell me about a project where the scope changed mid-way
- How do you prioritize competing research requests?

### Leadership & Mentoring
- Tell me about a time you mentored someone or improved a team process
- Describe how you've influenced research methodology at your company
- How do you decide what NOT to research?

### Failure & Learning
- Tell me about a research project that didn't go as planned
- Describe a time you made a mistake in your analysis
- What would you do differently if you could redo a past project?

## Story Bank Template

For each story, prepare:

```
TITLE: [Short name for the story]
COMPETENCY TAGS: [impact, collaboration, ambiguity, leadership, failure]
SITUATION: [2 sentences]
TASK: [1 sentence — your specific role]
ACTION: [3–5 bullet points of specific steps you took]
RESULT: [Quantified outcome + learning]
FLEX: [Other questions this story can answer]
```

## Meta-Tips

- **Listen for the real question** — "Tell me about a time you dealt with conflict" is really about emotional intelligence, not the conflict itself
- **End with the learning** — shows growth mindset
- **Have a "failure" story ready** — they WILL ask; a good failure story shows self-awareness
- **Ask clarifying questions** — "Would you like me to focus on the technical or stakeholder aspect?"
