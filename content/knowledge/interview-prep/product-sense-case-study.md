Frameworks for product sense and case study questions in UXR and data science interviews.

## The Product Sense Framework (RICE-like)

When asked "How would you measure / research / evaluate X?":

### Step 1: Clarify
- What is the product / feature?
- Who are the users?
- What's the business goal?
- What decisions will this research inform?

### Step 2: Structure the Problem
- What are the key dimensions to evaluate?
- What does "success" look like?
- What are the biggest unknowns?

### Step 3: Propose a Research Approach
- Method selection (and WHY this method)
- Sample / data source
- Key metrics or constructs to measure
- Timeline and constraints

### Step 4: Anticipate Trade-Offs
- What are the limitations of this approach?
- What would you do with more time/resources?
- What could go wrong?

## Common Product Sense Questions

### Measurement Questions
- "How would you measure the success of [feature X]?"
- Framework: Define success dimensions → Choose metrics → Identify data sources → Propose tracking plan
- Key: distinguish between **leading** (early signal) and **lagging** (outcome) metrics

### Diagnostic Questions
- "Feature X launched and engagement dropped. What would you do?"
- Framework: Segment → Hypothesize → Test → Recommend
  1. Segment the drop (by user type, platform, market, time)
  2. Generate hypotheses (novelty wore off, bug, cannibalization, seasonal)
  3. Design analysis to test each hypothesis
  4. Recommend next steps

### Trade-Off Questions
- "Should we optimize for engagement or satisfaction?"
- Framework: Define the tension → Show data approach → Propose resolution
- Key insight: these are often false dichotomies — look for the interaction

### Design Evaluation Questions
- "Here's a design mockup. How would you evaluate it?"
- Framework: Heuristic review → User study design → Metrics → Iteration plan

## UXR-Specific Case Study Tips

1. **Start with the user, not the metric** — "First, I'd want to understand who uses this and why"
2. **Show methodological breadth** — mention both qual and quant approaches
3. **Acknowledge constraints** — timeline, sample access, resource limits
4. **Connect to decisions** — "This would help the team decide whether to..."
5. **Think about validity** — "One risk is that we'd be measuring stated preference rather than actual behavior"

## Practice Structure (15 minutes per question)

| Phase | Time | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| Clarify | 2 min | Ask 2–3 targeted questions |
| Structure | 3 min | Lay out your framework on paper/whiteboard |
| Deep dive | 7 min | Walk through your approach in detail |
| Trade-offs | 3 min | Discuss limitations, alternatives, "what if" scenarios |
