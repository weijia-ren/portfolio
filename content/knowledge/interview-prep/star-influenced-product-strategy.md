**Interview themes**: Impact, stakeholder influence, strategic thinking, data-driven decisions

## Situation

<!-- Fill in your real example -->

A product team was planning their H2 roadmap based primarily on feature requests from vocal users and competitive analysis. There was no systematic evidence about which problems were most common or most painful for the broader user base.

## Task

I needed to provide evidence-based input that could redirect the roadmap toward the highest-impact opportunities, without alienating the team or dismissing their existing priorities.

## Action

1. Proposed and ran a mixed-methods study combining behavioral log analysis (identifying where users struggled) with a prioritization survey (N=1,200) measuring problem frequency and severity
2. Created a 2x2 impact matrix plotting problem frequency vs. user-reported severity
3. Identified 3 "hidden high-impact" problems that were frequent in logs but absent from feature requests
4. Presented findings in a workshop format (not just a readout) so the team could interact with the data
5. Worked with the PM to integrate findings into the prioritization framework

## Result

- 2 of the 3 hidden problems were added to the H2 roadmap
- One became the #1 priority, resulting in a [X]% improvement in [metric]
- The frequency-severity framework was adopted as a standard input for roadmap planning
- The PM cited this research in their PSC narrative as a key input

## Key talking points

- Demonstrates research → strategy influence (not just "did a study")
- Shows diplomatic stakeholder management (workshop, not lecture)
- Combines quant methods (logs + survey) with practical impact
- Reusable framework, not one-off
