## The Pyramid Principle (Barbara Minto)

**Lead with the answer, then support with evidence.** Not the other way around.

### Anti-Pattern (how most researchers present)
"We did X methodology, then Y analysis, then found Z."
вҶ’ Audience is lost before you get to the point.

### Better Pattern
"We should do Z. Here's why: Finding 1, Finding 2, Finding 3. Here's the evidence."
вҶ’ Audience knows where you're going and can evaluate evidence in context.

## The Research Readout Template

### 1. The Hook (30 seconds)
- One sentence: what did we study and why does it matter?
- "We investigated why [problem] because it affects [business outcome]."

### 2. The Bottom Line (1 minute)
- 2вҖ“3 key findings вҖ” the "so what"
- State the recommendation or decision implication upfront

### 3. The Evidence (5вҖ“10 minutes)
- Walk through findings with supporting data
- One chart per finding, clearly labeled
- Use the "headline + evidence" format for each slide

### 4. Implications & Next Steps (2 minutes)
- What should the team do with this?
- What's still unknown?
- What's the recommended next research?

### 5. Appendix
- Methodology details, sample description, additional analyses
- Available for questions but don't present proactively

## Slide Design Rules

| Rule | Why |
|------|-----|
| **One idea per slide** | Cognitive load; audience can only process one thing at a time |
| **Headline = the finding** (not the topic) | "Users prefer video for entertainment searches" beats "Video preference analysis" |
| **Chart title = the takeaway** | "Satisfaction drops above 3 video results" beats "Video density vs. satisfaction" |
| **Minimize text** | If you can say it, don't write it |
| **Annotate charts** | Call out the key data point directly on the chart |

## Adapting to Your Audience

| Audience | What they care about | Adjust by... |
|----------|---------------------|-------------|
| **Executives** | Decision, impact, risk | Lead with recommendation, skip methodology, quantify impact |
| **Product managers** | Actionability, roadmap implications | Connect findings to specific product levers |
| **Engineers** | Feasibility, specificity | Be precise about what needs to change technically |
| **Designers** | User experience, design implications | Use quotes, journey examples, visual evidence |
| **Other researchers** | Methodology, validity, rigor | Include analytical details, discuss limitations |

## Handling Pushback

| Pushback type | Response strategy |
|---|---|
| "The sample is too small" | Explain statistical power, show confidence intervals, acknowledge limitations |
| "That's not what we see in the data" | Explore the discrepancy together вҖ” different metrics may tell different stories |
| "We already knew that" | Reframe: "The value is in the quantification and nuance вҖ” here's what's new" |
| "We can't act on this" | Ask: "What would make it actionable?" and adjust future research accordingly |
| "Can we trust the methodology?" | Walk through design choices and validation; offer to share details offline |
