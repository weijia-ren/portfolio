A decision framework for choosing the right UXR method.

## The 2×2: Behavioral vs. Attitudinal × Qualitative vs. Quantitative

|  | **Qualitative** | **Quantitative** |
|---|---|---|
| **Behavioral** (what people do) | Usability testing, field studies, diary studies, eye tracking | A/B testing, log analysis, clickstream, funnel analysis |
| **Attitudinal** (what people say/think) | Interviews, focus groups, card sorting, concept testing | Surveys, unmoderated testing at scale, desirability studies |

## Method Selection by Research Question

| Question Type | Methods | When |
|---|---|---|
| **"What's happening?"** (Discovery) | Interviews, field studies, diary studies, log analysis | Early stage, understanding the problem space |
| **"Why is it happening?"** (Explanation) | In-depth interviews, contextual inquiry, survey follow-ups, mixed-methods | After identifying a pattern, need to understand drivers |
| **"How well does it work?"** (Evaluation) | Usability testing, heuristic evaluation, benchmarking surveys | Evaluating a design or product |
| **"Which is better?"** (Comparison) | A/B testing, preference testing, within-subjects experiments | Choosing between design options |
| **"How much / how many?"** (Measurement) | Surveys, analytics, longitudinal tracking | Quantifying behaviors, attitudes, or outcomes |

## Core Methods — Quick Reference

### Qualitative

| Method | N | Duration | Best for |
|--------|---|----------|----------|
| **In-Depth Interviews (IDIs)** | 8–15 | 45–90 min | Deep understanding of motivations, mental models |
| **Contextual Inquiry** | 6–12 | 1–2 hours | Understanding behavior in natural context |
| **Usability Testing** | 5–8 | 30–60 min | Identifying usability issues, task completion |
| **Diary Studies** | 10–20 | 1–4 weeks | Longitudinal behavior, habits, routines |
| **Card Sorting** | 15–30 | 20–40 min | Information architecture, categorization |
| **Concept Testing** | 8–15 | 30–60 min | Early-stage reaction to ideas before building |

### Quantitative

| Method | N | Duration | Best for |
|--------|---|----------|----------|
| **Surveys** | 200+ | 5–15 min | Measuring attitudes, preferences at scale |
| **A/B Testing** | 1000+ | 1–4 weeks | Causal impact of design changes |
| **Log/Behavioral Analysis** | All users | Ongoing | Understanding actual behavior patterns |
| **Unmoderated Testing** | 50–200 | 10–20 min | Scalable usability benchmarking |
| **Tree Testing** | 50+ | 10–15 min | Validating information architecture |
| **MaxDiff / Conjoint** | 200+ | 15–20 min | Feature prioritization, trade-off analysis |

## Mixed Methods — Integration Patterns

| Pattern | How it works | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Exploratory Sequential** | Qual → Quant | Interviews identify themes → survey validates at scale |
| **Explanatory Sequential** | Quant → Qual | Survey identifies patterns → interviews explain why |
| **Convergent Parallel** | Qual + Quant simultaneously | Survey + interviews on the same topic, triangulate |
| **Embedded** | One method nested within another | Survey with open-text responses analyzed qualitatively |

## Study Quality Checklist

- [ ] Clear research questions tied to product decisions
- [ ] Method matches the question type
- [ ] Sample is appropriate for the method and goals
- [ ] Analysis plan defined before data collection
- [ ] Findings are actionable, not just interesting
- [ ] Limitations are acknowledged
