A framework for evaluating and communicating the impact of research work. Useful for PSC, project planning, and stakeholder conversations.

## The Four Levels of Research Impact

| Level | What it means | Example |
|-------|--------------|---------|
| **Inform** | Research surfaced a new insight | "We discovered that 40% of users don't understand the setting" |
| **Influence** | Research changed a decision | "Based on findings, the team pivoted from approach A to B" |
| **Shape** | Research defined the strategy | "The research roadmap drove the H2 product strategy" |
| **Transform** | Research changed how the org works | "The measurement framework is now used across all product teams" |

## How to use this

- When scoping a project, aim for at least "Influence" level impact
- When writing up results, explicitly state which level of impact was achieved
- In PSC narratives, frame your strongest work at the highest applicable level
- Not every project needs to be "Transform" — consistent "Influence" is excellent

## Choosing the right level

Before starting a project, ask:

1. What decision will this research inform?
2. Who is the decision-maker?
3. What will change if we find X vs. Y?

If you can't answer these, the project risks staying at "Inform" level — interesting but not impactful.
